# Demo Script

Demo beats with talking points for the audience.

---

## Intro

Hi everyone, thank you for coming today. I'm excited to show you the power of long-running local agents with private data on DGX Spark.

I'll be showing a real-time vision-enabled chat agent running fully locally on Spark.

> **Quick disclaimer:** This is something I vibe coded over a weekend and is by no means a finished product — it just shows what's possible with Spark. Let's dive in.

---

## Beat 1: Realtime Vision *(desktop)*

- **U:** Hey! Can you see me?
- **A:** Yep. You're on camera, audio is clear, and I'm ready.

---

## Sketch → Frontend

### Beat 2: Prompt → MVP *(desktop)*

- **U:** Please develop a frontend for the chip selector harness I've been working on based on this napkin sketch. *[holds up sketch]*
- **A:** On it → opens HTML assistant

As a developer, having local vision-enabled agents unlocks rapid prototyping with sensitive code I wouldn't feel comfortable sharing in the cloud.

**Benefits:**
- **Privacy** — all data stays fully local, unlocking sensitive domains for agentic development.
- **Local preferences** — my style preferences, git hygiene, and product context all live locally on Spark. The necessary context is already present and just gets richer with time.

---

## Menu Ordering

Next, I'm off to dinner at Din Tai Fung.

I've been excited to try the flagship location, but the menu is all in Chinese, which I don't speak.

I could use Google Translate. But translation alone does not know what I *should* order.

Privately, I'm managing high blood pressure, which can be difficult while traveling internationally for work.

So I ask my agent.

### Beat 3: Menu Recommendation with Private Health Data *(mobile)*

- **U:** What should I order? *[uploads photo to Discord bot]*

It will take a sec to upload the photo due to network latency, and just like that it translates the menu and gives a recommendation for `___________` because it's less salty.

This isn't just a translation task — this is the privacy-first version of agentic intelligence: hyper-personalized agents I can trust with sensitive data for real-world decision making.

---

## Email Draft

Dinner went well and in the Uber back, I can use my Spark to send updates to my team.

### Beat 4: Email Draft (Productivity on the Go)

- **U:** Hey Claw, draft an email to the team saying the manufacturing partners from dinner loved the product pitch and asked to see our internal cost reduction benchmarks. Also let them know that I have a product launch website ready for review in our sync tomorrow.
- **A:** On it — drafting that now. Saved to `workspace/email-draft.md`.

With long-running local agents on Spark, I can translate natural language intention to agentic action on the go.

---

## Back Home

Once back home, let's check our agent's progress.

*[open workspace folder / email file]*

In a shared workspace folder, the agent wrote the email draft — highlighting next steps, review items, etc.

**Result:** my team stays in sync, context grows richer, and development is accelerated across the startup stack.

---

## Personal To-Do

Thanks to the time I saved, tomorrow is a free day to explore Taipei. One last thing —

### Beat 5: Long-Context Agentic Memory

- **U:** Add a personal to-do to get my husband pineapple cakes.
- **A:** You got him that last year. Want me to add the high mountain oolong tea instead?
- **U:** Yes please, thanks Claw.

This is the power of long-context agentic memory — my agent recalls a random purchase from a year ago to help me make a more informed decision today.

While I'm using this in a personal context, the implications for enterprise are significant. I wouldn't feel comfortable uploading purchase data to the cloud for fear of getting customized ads or other reasons, but with Spark, I'm able to leverage agentic intelligence over time to synthesize my history and improve outcomes.
